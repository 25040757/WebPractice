Beef Stew

Stew beef (or stew meat) consists of small, cubed pieces of beef, typically cut from tougher, collagen-rich muscles like the chuck or round, designed for slow-cooking in liquid. When simmered over low heat, these cuts become tender and flavorful, making them ideal for stews, soups, and chili.

Ingridents

  • 1 Tbsp. (or more) vegetable oil
  • 2 lb. beef chuck stew meat, cut into 1" cubes
  • 1 medium yellow onion
  • 2 carrots, peeled, cut into rounds
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 6 cups low-sodium beef broth
  • cup red wine
  • 1 Tbsp. Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 fresh thyme sprigs
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 lb. baby potatoes, halved
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Coat bottom of pot with oil if needed and heat over medium-high heat. Cook onion, carrots, and celery, stirring occasionally, until softened, about 7 minutes; season with salt and pepper. Add garlic and tomato paste and cook, stirring, until garlic is fragrant and tomato paste is darkened, about 2 minutes. Return beef and any accumulated juices to pot. Add broth, wine, Worcestershire, thyme, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low and bring to a simmer; season with salt and pepper. Cover and simmer, stirring occasionally, until beef is tender, 30 to 45 minutes.
    2. Add potatoes and simmer, covered, until potatoes are tender, about 15 minutes.
  2. Remove bay leaves and thyme. Stir in peas and cook, stirring, until warmed through, about 2 minutes; season with salt and pepper.
  3. Divide stew among bowls. Top with parsley.
